“Reason is the natural order of truth; but imagination is the organ of meaning.”
C.S. Lewis

Probably best known as the author of the beloved children's fantasy series The Chronicles of Narnia, Clive Staples Lewis (1898-1963) was one of the most influential minds of the 20th century. A chair and professor of literature at both Oxford and Cambridge University, Lewis produced an enormous breadth of fiction and non-fiction that continues to be read and appreciated by a wide audience. In addition to his Narnia books, some of Lewis' most enduring works include Till We Have Faces, The Screwtape Letters, Mere Christianity and Out of the Silent Planet.
